# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two audiences, roughly equal weight:

- **Locals and regulars** — already know or have heard of Âmbar. They visit to check the menu, confirm hours, or make a reservation. They arrive with intent; the site needs to be efficient without feeling transactional.
- **Tourists and first-time discoverers** — finding a dinner option for tonight, or researching the city's fine-dining scene. They arrive without trust. The site's primary job for them is to communicate atmosphere and earn the reservation before they've set foot inside.

Both audiences are decision-makers with high expectations. Neither is browsing casually.

## Product Purpose

Âmbar's website is the restaurant's digital face before a guest walks through the door. It exists to:

1. Make a strong first impression that matches the in-room experience.
2. Let visitors find what they need — menu, hours, reservation — without friction.
3. Communicate the restaurant's personality without needing to explain it.

Success: a visitor leaves either with a reservation or with a clear, specific impression of what kind of restaurant Âmbar is.

## Brand Personality

**Three words:** Luminous. Deliberate. Unhurried.

Âmbar is a contemporary fine-dining restaurant. It's warm without being rustic, precise without being cold. The reference is Eleven Madison Park: confident, ingredient-forward, generous with space and attention, never austere or stiff. The name itself — amber — signals warmth, clarity, depth. The brass accent in the visual language carries that warmth; the background and typography hold it in check.

Voice: measured and assured. Sentences that breathe. No exclamation points, no superlatives, no invitation to "explore." The food speaks; the copy frames.

## Anti-references

- **OpenTable / generic booking aesthetics** — functional, transactional, zero personality. Listing restaurants like a directory. No sense of what makes a place worth choosing.
- **Rustic / farmhouse visual language** — wood textures, warm creams, terracotta, serif-on-beige. The Italian trattoria default. The name "Âmbar" and the fine-dining positioning actively contradict this.
- **Influencer food blog aesthetics** — blush pink, rounded cards, casual voice, heavy use of gradients and pastel. Nothing of this caliber of restaurant belongs in that register.
- **The cream / near-white warm body background** — the 2026 saturated AI default. Warmth is carried by the brass accent and the typeface choice, not by tinting the background.

## Design Principles

1. **The dish is the protagonist.** Photography does the emoting. The site is a frame, not a gallery wall. Nothing decorative competes with the food.
2. **Earn every pixel.** If an element doesn't serve the guest directly, it doesn't exist. No section headers that say "Our Story." No icon arrays. No shadow cards.
3. **Warmth without cliché.** The brass accent and the soft-serif typeface carry warmth. The background is dark and contained. Never tinted neutral "to feel warm."
4. **Generous space as a value statement.** A fine-dining restaurant does not rush its guests. The layout should not rush the visitor. Editorial spacing, deliberate pacing.
5. **Confident understatement.** The site knows who it is. No superlatives. No explanations. Visitors are trusted to get it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **Target:** WCAG AA
- Body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast; large text ≥ 3:1. Extra care on light text over dark backgrounds — they often appear lower contrast than measured.
- Full keyboard navigation (focus rings visible against the dark background).
- `prefers-reduced-motion` respected: every entrance animation has a zero-motion fallback. Reduced motion is not optional.
- Alt text written in the brand voice, not as descriptions ("Torched duck breast, foie gras terrine, plum reduction" not "image of duck").
